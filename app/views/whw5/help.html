Self-isolation

Help and support while self-isolating

While you're self-isolating:

  • You might be able to get sick pay or other types of financial support if you're not able to work
  • You can get help with everyday tasks, like collecting shopping or medicines, from an NHS volunteer

Financial support

If you're on a low income and you're asked to self-isolate by NHS Test and Trace, you may be able to apply for a £500 Test and Trace Support Payment from your local council.

There is a lot of information to help you during this period:

  • Coronavirus (COVID-19): what to do if you're employed and cannot work
  • Local Authority and Council support in your area

Help with everyday tasks from an NHS volunteer.

NHS Volunteer Responders can help with things like:

  • collecting shopping
  • collecting medicines and prescriptions
  • phone calls if you want to chat to someone

Call 0808 196 3646 (8am to 8pm, everyday) to arrange help from a volunteer.

Get coronavirus help from your local council.

You might be able to get:

  • money to help with living costs
  • help getting food and other essentials
  • support for your business

Find out what help you can get from your local council if you're affected by coronavirus.

Emotional support

It's important to remember to take care of your mind as well as your body and to get support if you need it. Stay in touch with family and friends over the phone or on social media. There are also sources of support and information that can help.

Staying at home and self-isolating can be difficult, stressful or inconvenient but by self-isolating you're saving lives.

Information:

Self-isolating protects your family, friends and local community and breaks the chain of transmission. You could be fined if you do not self-isolate.
