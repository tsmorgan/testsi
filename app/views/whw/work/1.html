NHS Covid-19

Advice if you're employed, self-employed or a job seeker

Advice for employees

Tell your employer if you cannot work while you're self-isolating.

You should work from home if you can and are well enough.

You do not need to get a note from a GP.

You can get an isolation note to send to your employer as proof you need to be off work.

Financial support if you cannot work

Your employer should tell you if you're covered by their sick leave or special leave policy.

If you cannot get sick pay from your employer, you might be able to get Statutory Sick Pay or another type of financial support.

If you're on a low income and you're asked to self-isolate by NHS Test and Trace, you may be able to apply for a £500 Test and Trace Support Payment from your local council.

If you cannot work from home, you might be able to get:

  • a one off payment if you've been told to self-isolate - there are different schemes in England, Northern Ireland, Scotland and Wales
  • Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) from your employer
  • New Style Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) if you cannot get SSP, you're under State Pension age, and you have made enough National Insurance contributions over the last 2 to 3 years - you could also get it if your child has symptoms or is self-isolating
  • Universal Credit if you or your partner are under State Pension age and you have less than £16,000 in savings - you might be able to get it at the same time as SSP or New Style ESA
  • Pension Credit if both you and your partner have reached State Pension age - you might be able to get it at the same time as SSP

There is a lot of information to help you during this period:

  • Coronavirus (COVID-19): what to do if you're employed and cannot workfrom your employer
  • Local Authority and Council support in your area

Advice if you're self-employed

Financial support

You may be entitled to a one-off payment of £500 through the NHS Test and Trace Support Payment scheme if you are required to stay at home and self-isolate. You can apply for the NHS Test and Trace Support Payment online or through the NHS COVID-19 app.

If you or your child is self-isolating you might be able to get New Style Employment and Support Allowance (ESA).

Advice if you're a job seeker

You could be eligible for a £500 Test and Trace Support Payment if you live in England and you're currently receiving or are the partner of someone in the same household who is receiving, at least one of the following benefits:

  • Universal Credit
  • Working Tax Credit
  • Income-based Employment and Support Allowance
  • Iincome-based Jobseeker's Allowance
  • Income Support
  • Housing Benefit
  • Pension Credit
