NHS Covid-19

You have been told to self isolate by the NHS Covid-19 App

Until 17 December, the NHS COVID-19 app was designed to tell you to self-isolate for 14 days if you'd been in close contact with someone who has tested positive for coronavirus (COVID-19).

Policy in England and Wales recently changed to make this 10 days instead. Read more about the self-isolation period in Wales. Read more about the self-isolation period in England. The app was updated to reflect this on 17 December.

If you received a close contact alert on or before 17 December, the self-isolation timer on your app may still be telling you to self-isolate for longer than you need to. This is only if you're self-isolating because you've been in close contact with someone who has tested positive for coronavirus, not if you're self-isolating because you have symptoms or have tested positive yourself. Read about what this alert will look like.

If you got this alert on 16 December or earlier

You can leave self-isolation when your timer says '3 days to go', unless you've been told to self-isolate for longer by contact tracing. This is when your self-isolation period of 10 full days will be complete.

If you got this alert on 17 December

You will need to check your 'encounter date' in the 'manage my data' section of the app. Read more about how to find your encounter date. You need to self-isolate for 10 full days from this date.

For example, if your encounter date was 10 December, you would need to self-isolate up to and including 20 December. You could then stop self-isolating on 21 December.

If you get this alert on 18 December or later

You should self-isolate for the full time indicated by the app.

More in When to self-isolate

  • You have symptoms that might mean you have Covid-19
  • You have tested positive for Covid-19
  • You have been told to self-isolate by NHS Test and Trace
  • You have been told to self isolate by the NHS Covid-19 App
  • The NHS Covid-19 App has advised you that you have visited a venue whilst some with Covid-19 was there.
