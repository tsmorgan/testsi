Self-isolation

Get help and financial support while self-isolating

Staying at home and self-isolating can be difficult but it's important to stop coronavirus (COVID-19) spreading to other people.

Help and support is available while you're at home.

Financial support

If you're on a low income and you're asked to self-isolate by NHS Test and Trace, you may be able to apply for a £500 Test and Trace Support Payment on GOV.UK.

There’s separate information about self-isolation and work.

Help with everyday tasks from an NHS volunteer

NHS Volunteer Responders can help with things like:

  • collecting shopping
  • collecting medicines and prescriptions
  • phone calls if you want to chat to someone

Call 0808 196 3646 (8am to 8pm, everyday) to arrange help from a volunteer.

Get help from your local council

You might be able to get:

  • money to help with living costs
  • help getting food and other essentials
  • support for your business

Get coronavirus help from your local council on GOV.UK.

Emotional support

It's important to remember to take care of your mind as well as your body and to get support if you need it.

Every Mind Matters has some useful tips to help your mental wellbeing while staying at home.
