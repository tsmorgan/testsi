Self-isolation

Why it’s important to self-isolate

Self-isolating when you have coronavirus (COVID-19) or have been in close contact with someone who has the virus can stop it spreading to other people.

Coronavirus can spread though:

  • coughs and sneezes
  • tiny droplets when you breathe or speak
  • touching objects and surfaces with the virus on them

People who have coronavirus can infect others from around 2 days before their symptoms start, and for up to 10 days after. Self-isolating for 10 full days can help stop the spread.

Why you still need to self-isolate if you do not have symptoms

It’s important to self-isolate even if you feel well.

It can take several days for symptoms of coronavirus to appear. You could have the virus but not know it yet.

About 1 in 3 people with the virus do not get symptoms, but can still spread it to other people.

Why the people you live with are asked to self-isolate

Coronavirus spreads more easily when people are close to each other, especially when they’re inside without regular fresh air.

If you have coronavirus, any people you live with are at high risk of getting it too.

They could spread it to others even if they feel well, which is why they may also be asked to self-isolate.

Self-isolation is a legal requirement

As self-isolation is so important, it's a legal requirement to do it if you test positive or are told to by NHS Test and Trace. You could be fined if you do not self-isolate.
