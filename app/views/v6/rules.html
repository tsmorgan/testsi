Self-isolation

The rules of self-isolation

Self-isolation is when you do not leave your home because you have or might have coronavirus (COVID-19). This helps stop the virus spreading to other people.

You should self-isolate immediately if you test positive for coronavirus. It's a legal requirement to self-isolate if you are told by NHS Test and Trace. You could be fined if you do not self-isolate.

There’s separate information about when to self-isolate.

Self-isolation is different to:

  • social distancing – general advice for everyone to avoid close contact with other people
  • shielding – advice for people at high risk from coronavirus

What you can and cannot do when self-isolating

Don't

  • do not go to work, school or public places

  • do not go out to get food and medicine

  • do not have visitors in your home, including friends and family - except for people providing essential care

  • do not go out to exercise

  • do not go on public transport or use taxis

  • do not take your dog out for a walk - ask someone outside your household or support bubble to walk your dog for you

Do

  • tell your employer if you cannot work from home

  • order food and medicine online or by phone, or ask someone to bring it to your home

  • exercise at home or in your garden if you have one

When you can leave home while self-isolating

You must not leave or be outside of your home except where you have a “reasonable excuse” including:

  • getting medical help if you're seriously ill - call 111 if you're unsure what to do
  • escaping abuse
  • avoiding a risk of harm

Read more about the “reasonable excuses” in the law on legislation.gov.uk.
