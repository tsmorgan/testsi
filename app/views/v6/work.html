Self-isolation and work

Advice for employees

You should work from home if you can and are well enough.

Tell your employer if you cannot work while you're self-isolating.

You can get an isolation note to send to your employer as proof you need to be off work.

You do not need to get a note from a GP.

Get an isolation note

Financial support if you cannot work

Your employer should tell you if you're covered by their sick leave or special leave policy. If you cannot get sick pay from your employer, you might be able to get Statutory Sick Pay (SSP). Find out about Statutory Sick Pay on GOV.UK

If you're asked to self-isolate by NHS Test and Trace, you may be able to get a £500 Test and Trace Support Payment from your local council if you live in England.

You may be eligible if:

  • you’re usually working and are on low income
  • you’re usually working and get benefits
Apply for a Test and Trace Support Payment on GOV.UK

Support schemes in other parts of the UK:

  • GOV.WALES: self-isolation support scheme
  • mygov.scot: self-isolation support grant
  • nidirect: extra financial support

You might be eligible for other types of financial support.

Find out more about what to do if you’re employed and cannot work on GOV.UK

Advice if you're self-employed

You may be entitled to a one-off payment of £500 through the Test and Trace Support Payment scheme if you need to stay at home and self-isolate.

You can apply for the Test and Trace Support Payment on GOV.UK or through the NHS COVID-19 app.

Find out more about how to apply for the Test and Trace payment through the NHS COVID-19 app.

If you or your child is self-isolating you might be able to get New Style Employment and Support Allowance (ESA).

Find out more about New Style and Employment and Support Allowance on GOV.UK.

Advice if you’re looking for work

You could be eligible for a £500 Test and Trace Support Payment if you live in England and you're currently receiving or are the partner of someone in the same household who is receiving, at least one of the following benefits:

  • Universal Credit
  • Working Tax Credit
  • income-based Employment and Support Allowance
  • income-based Jobseeker's Allowance
  • Income Support
  • Housing Benefit
  • Pension Credit

If you’re not on one of these benefits you could be eligible for a £500 discretionary payment.

Advice for employers

Make sure your workers self-isolate

You should ask your employees to get an isolation note if you need evidence they’ve been asked to self-isolate. You may need this evidence to reclaim SSP.

Financial support for employees

Employees in self-isolation are entitled to Statutory Sick Pay for every day they are in isolation, as long as they meet the eligibility conditions. You may be able to claim back Statutory Sick Pay paid to your employees due to coronavirus.

Find out about claiming back Statutory Sick Pay on GOV.UK
